🌟 Key Notes on Differentiation

📌 What is Differentiation?

Differentiation is a teaching approach where educators proactively plan and implement varied approaches to content, process, and product in anticipation of and in response to student differences in readiness, interests, and learning needs.

📚 Content Differentiation

What it means: Varying what students learn or how they access information

  • ✓ Provide texts at different reading levels
  • ✓ Use multimedia resources (videos, audio)
  • ✓ Offer choice in topics to study
  • ✓ Pre-teach vocabulary for struggling learners
  • ✓ Provide extension materials for advanced students
Download Guide
⚙️ Process Differentiation

What it means: Varying how students make sense of ideas and information

  • ✓ Tiered activities at different complexity levels
  • ✓ Learning stations with varied tasks
  • ✓ Flexible grouping strategies
  • ✓ Choice boards for activities
  • ✓ Varied time allowances for tasks
Download Guide
📝 Product Differentiation

What it means: Varying how students demonstrate what they've learned

  • ✓ Choice in assessment formats
  • ✓ Projects, presentations, or written work
  • ✓ Creative demonstrations of learning
  • ✓ Varied rubrics for different products
  • ✓ Student choice in showing mastery
Download Guide
🎯 Learning Environment

What it means: Creating a supportive classroom climate

  • ✓ Flexible seating arrangements
  • ✓ Safe space for risk-taking
  • ✓ Clear routines and expectations
  • ✓ Respect for all learners
  • ✓ Growth mindset culture
Download Guide

💡 Key Principles of Differentiation:

  • ✓ Start with clear learning objectives
  • ✓ Know your students' readiness, interests, and learning profiles
  • ✓ Use ongoing assessment to inform instruction
  • ✓ Provide multiple pathways to learning
  • ✓ Maintain high expectations for all students
  • ✓ Create a respectful and supportive community
